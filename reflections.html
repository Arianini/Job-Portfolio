Reflection: My Journey Through SAS3000 and Beyond

Insights on growth, self-awareness, and career aspirations.

Masid-Danas: Observations and Self-Discoveries

My experience in SAS3000 was transformative. It wasn’t just a course—it was a mirror reflecting my strengths, weaknesses, and untapped potential. Through the modules, I discovered my resilience in tackling complex problems, my passion for innovation, and my need to embrace vulnerability. I observed how curiosity fueled my learning and how collaboration enriched my insights. This journey was also a humbling reminder that growth is not linear; every challenge I encountered served as a stepping stone to deeper self-awareness.

I realized that my analytical mindset, a key asset as a future cybersecurity engineer, can also be my limitation if I overthink. Learning to balance my technical focus with human-centric thinking has been eye-opening. It’s a reminder that cybersecurity is not just about securing systems but protecting people, their stories, and their livelihoods.

Suri-Nilay: Influence of Past and Present on My Career Pursuit

Looking back, my journey has been shaped by a blend of aspirations, failures, and reflections. My past taught me the value of persistence and adaptability. Growing up in a digitally evolving world ignited my fascination with cybersecurity. Witnessing data breaches and the devastating impacts of cybercrime instilled in me a sense of purpose—to build a career that safeguards individuals and organizations.

However, my values, attitudes, and assumptions are not without their biases. I’ve often equated success with perfection, which sometimes paralyzed me from taking risks. This realization helped me see that vulnerability is a strength, not a flaw. My present mindset is about progress, not perfection. I am learning to embrace discomfort because it’s where true growth happens.

Taya-Kilos: What I Want to Change and Improve

I want to transform my self-doubt into self-belief. There are moments when I question my capabilities, and while it keeps me grounded, it can also limit me. I want to cultivate a mindset that embraces mistakes as opportunities for learning, not indicators of failure.

Another area I want to improve is my communication skills. As a cybersecurity engineer, technical expertise is essential, but so is the ability to translate complex ideas into accessible language. This is crucial for building trust with non-technical stakeholders and driving impactful change in the organizations I will serve.

Finally, I want to be more intentional about balancing my career ambitions with personal well-being. Burnout is a reality in any demanding field, and I aspire to sustain my passion without compromising my health or relationships.

Ramdam-Pakiramdam: Implications for Myself and Others

The actions I take today have a ripple effect on my future career, myself, and the people around me. Every effort to learn, every mistake I embrace, and every relationship I nurture shapes the professional I am becoming. As a future cybersecurity engineer, my decisions will not only impact systems but also influence trust and safety in the digital world.

For myself, my actions are a declaration of my commitment to lifelong learning and ethical practice. For others, they are a promise that I will approach my work with empathy, integrity, and a dedication to making technology a safer space for everyone.

In pursuing this path, I understand that I carry the responsibility of inspiring others—be it peers, mentors, or future generations of cybersecurity professionals. I want to be a beacon of hope and a testament to the idea that change starts with intention and action.
